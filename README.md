# Apollo 11 Mission Documentation

## Overview
Apollo 11 was the historic American spaceflight that first landed humans on the Moon.  Commander **Neil Armstrong**, Lunar Module Pilot **Buzz Aldrin**, and Command Module Pilot **Michael Collins** launched from Kennedy Space Center on **16 July 1969** aboard the Saturn V rocket.  The Lunar Module **Eagle** touched down in the Sea of Tranquility on **20 July 1969** (UTC 20:17), where Armstrong famously said, “That’s one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind.”  Aldrin joined him shortly after, and together they spent **21 hours 36 minutes** on the lunar surface, collecting 21.5 kg of samples.

The primary objective, set by President John F. Kennedy in 1961, was to perform a crewed lunar landing and safely return the crew to Earth, which was achieved on **24 July 1969** with splashdown in the Pacific Ocean.

### Recent Highlights (2024)
In 2024, NASA commemorated the 55th anniversary of the landing with live broadcasts, restored footage, and new high‑resolution photos, linking the legacy of Apollo 11 to the ongoing Artemis program.

### Why It Matters
Apollo 11 fulfilled the national goal of landing a man on the Moon before the end of the decade, marking the end of the Space Race and demonstrating the capabilities of human space exploration.
